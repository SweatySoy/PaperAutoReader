### 1. Quantum Simulations of Hadron Dynamics in the Schwinger Model using 112 Qubits
**Physical Review D** | 2024-01-16 | [链接](https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.08044v2)

> ⚡ **影响力来源**: Lower relevance (47.9) but high impact (70.0) - cross-domain insight.

> 💡 **跨界启发**: This paper demonstrates the first large-scale quantum simulation of hadron physics (subatomic particle interactions) using 112 qubits on IBM's Heron quantum computer. The team achieved something remarkable: they prepared a localized hadron 'wavepacket' on a quantum lattice, then watched it propagate through spacetime for 14 time steps. The key insight is that they exploit a physics phenomenon called 'confinement' - where particles are bound together at long distances - to dramatically simplify the quantum circuits needed. This simplification makes the simulation tractable on current hardware. The work demonstrates that near-term quantum computers can solve problems in high-energy physics that would be classically intractable, suggesting quantum advantage is approaching for certain physics problems.
